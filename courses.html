Essential Maintenance Schedule

Regular maintenance of safety equipment and systems is crucial for ensuring workplace safety and compliance. Below is our recommended maintenance schedule for key safety items.

Item Maintenance Focus
Emergency Lighting Test monthly; replace batteries and bulbs as needed.
First Aid Kits Inspect contents monthly; restock expired or used items.
Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) Clean, inspect, and replace damaged gear (e.g., gloves, helmets, goggles).
Ventilation Systems Clean filters and ducts; ensure airflow meets indoor air quality standards.
Electrical Panels Keep clear of obstructions; inspect for signs of wear or overheating.
Machine Guards Check for secure attachment and functionality; repair or replace if damaged.
Spill Kits Verify contents and accessibility; replace used or expired materials.

Maintenance Best Practices

  • Document all maintenance activities with dates and personnel involved
  • Schedule regular maintenance audits to ensure compliance
  • Train designated staff on proper maintenance procedures
  • Keep replacement parts and supplies readily available
  • Establish a reporting system for equipment issues
